About

I'm a BEng Electrical and Electronic Engineering graduate from Manchester Metropolitan University (2:1), with a strong foundation across power engineering, embedded systems, control theory, and analogue and digital electronics.

My final-year dissertation built a physics-based computer model of a lithium-ion battery under fast charging — linking charging rate to internal temperature rise and battery aging through a CC-CV current profile, Arrhenius-type resistance, and a forward-Euler ODE solver written in Python. You can explore the simulation interactively on the Projects page.

Before graduating, I worked as an Electrical & Automation Engineering Intern at BYK Additives, gaining hands-on experience with PLCs and SCADA systems on a live Combined Heat and Power project. I also led teams to two award-winning engineering projects during my degree — an optimised ventilator manufacturing plant designed with Siemens Digital Enterprise Suite, and a line-following robot built on a Raspberry Pi.

Outside of engineering, I enjoy weightlifting, hiking, badminton, reading, and journaling.
